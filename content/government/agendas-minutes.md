## About Agendas and Minutes

**Agendas** outline the items to be discussed at each Council meeting and are posted at least 24 hours in advance of the meeting.

**Minutes** provide a record of Council's discussions and decisions. Minutes are approved at the next regular Council meeting before being posted publicly.

## Understanding Council Documents

### What's in an Agenda?

Council meeting agendas typically include:

- **Call to Order**: Meeting opening procedures
- **Adoption of Agenda**: Approval of items to be discussed
- **Adoption of Minutes**: Approval of previous meeting minutes
- **Delegation/Presentations**: Scheduled speakers
- **Reports**: Updates from administration and committees
- **Bylaws**: Proposed or amended bylaws for consideration
- **Business Arising**: Follow-up on previous matters
- **New Business**: New items for discussion or decision
- **Correspondence**: Letters or communications received
- **In Camera Items**: Confidential matters (if any)
- **Adjournment**: Close of meeting

### What's in the Minutes?

Meeting minutes provide a record of:

- Attendance (Mayor, Councillors, and staff present)
- Motions made and voting results
- Key points of discussion
- Decisions and directions given
- Action items and follow-up required

Minutes do not provide a verbatim transcript but capture the essential information and outcomes.

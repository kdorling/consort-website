The Municipal Services & Utilities department plays a vital role in maintaining the infrastructure and essential services that keep the Village of Consort safe, clean, and connected. Our team manages sidewalks, water and sewer systems, garbage collection, and road maintenance—including snow removal, street sweeping, line painting, and pothole repairs. We're committed to delivering reliable, responsive service to our residents and take pride in caring for the spaces we all share.

## What We Do

Municipal Services & Utilities is responsible for maintaining and operating the Village's core infrastructure:

- **Water & Sewer Systems**: Treatment, distribution, maintenance, and emergency repairs
- **Roads & Streets**: Maintenance, repairs, snow removal, street sweeping, and line painting
- **Garbage & Waste Collection**: Residential and commercial waste pickup and disposal
- **Sidewalks**: Maintenance, repairs, and snow clearing
- **Storm Water Management**: Drainage systems and flood mitigation
- **Street Lighting**: Maintenance and repairs
- **Parks Maintenance**: Grounds keeping and seasonal care (in coordination with Recreation Services)
- **Fleet & Equipment**: Maintaining Village vehicles and equipment

## Frequently Asked Questions

### When will my street be paved?

The Village develops a multi-year capital plan for road rehabilitation and paving. Check the Capital Investment Plan or contact us for information about specific streets.

### Why is there water running in the street?

We may be flushing water lines for maintenance or testing fire hydrants. If you see water running and suspect a leak, please report it to pw@consort.ca.

### Can I plant trees in the right-of-way?

Planting in municipal right-of-way requires Village approval. Contact pw@consort.ca before planting to ensure proper species selection and placement.

### Who is responsible for the approach (driveway access) to my property?

Property owners are generally responsible for maintaining their approach. Contact our office for specific information about repairs or modifications.

### What should I do with hazardous waste?

Hazardous waste should not be placed in regular garbage. Contact us for information about hazardous waste collection events or depot locations.

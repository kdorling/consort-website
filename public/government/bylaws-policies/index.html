Bylaws, Policies, and Strategic Plans

January 1, 2025

The Village of Consort operates under a framework of bylaws, policies, and strategic plans that guide decision-making, regulate community standards, and provide direction for municipal operations. All documents are available for public review.

Bylaws

Bylaws are local laws passed by Council that regulate various aspects of community life within the Village of Consort. All bylaws must comply with provincial legislation, particularly the Municipal Government Act.

Bylaw Information

How Bylaws Are Passed:

  1. First Reading: Council introduces the bylaw
  2. Second Reading: Council discusses and may amend the bylaw
  3. Public Hearing: (if required) Public input is heard
  4. Third Reading: Council gives final approval
  5. Signing: Mayor and CAO sign the bylaw into effect

Bylaw Enforcement: The Village’s Bylaw Enforcement Officer is responsible for ensuring compliance with Village bylaws. To report a bylaw concern, contact bylaw@consort.ca or [Phone Number].

Public Input

The Village of Consort values input from residents on bylaws, policies, and strategic planning.

Ways to Provide Input:

  • Attend public hearings when required for specific bylaws
  • Submit written comments to Council
  • Request to appear as a delegation at a Council meeting
  • Participate in community consultations and surveys
  • Contact your Mayor or Councillors directly

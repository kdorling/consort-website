How Meetings Work

Meeting Agenda

The agenda for each Council meeting is posted online and at the Village Office at least 24 hours before the meeting. The agenda outlines the items that will be discussed and any decisions to be made.

View Meeting Agendas ‚Üí

Public Attendance

All residents are welcome to attend Council meetings. Seating is available in the Council Chambers on a first-come, first-served basis.

What to expect:

  • Meetings typically last 1-3 hours depending on the agenda
  • Council follows established procedures and rules of order
  • There is usually an opportunity for public questions or comments
  • Some items may be discussed in closed (in-camera) session as permitted by legislation

Speaking at Council (Delegations)

Residents who wish to address Council on a specific topic can request to appear as a delegation.

How to request a delegation:

  1. Contact the Village Office at least 5 business days before the meeting
  2. Provide your name, contact information, and the topic you wish to discuss
  3. Submit any written materials or presentations you plan to share
  4. You will be notified if your request is approved and added to the agenda

Delegation guidelines:

  • Presentations are typically limited to 10-15 minutes
  • Council may ask questions for clarification
  • Council may not make a decision immediately but may refer the matter for further consideration

Contact: info@consort.ca or [Phone Number]

Public Participation

Question Period

Most Council meetings include a time for public questions or comments. This is your opportunity to:

  • Ask questions about Village operations or services
  • Share concerns or suggestions
  • Provide input on agenda items

Please be respectful and concise in your comments. The Mayor will manage the question period to ensure everyone has an opportunity to participate.

Public Hearings

When Council considers certain bylaws (such as land use bylaws or tax rate bylaws), a public hearing may be required. Public hearings provide a formal opportunity for residents to express their views before Council makes a decision.

Notice of public hearings will be advertised in advance as required by legislation.

Meeting Minutes

Minutes from Council meetings are prepared and made available to the public after they are approved by Council (typically at the next regular meeting).

Closed (In-Camera) Sessions

Council may occasionally meet in closed session to discuss confidential matters as permitted by the Municipal Government Act, such as:

  • Labour relations or employee matters
  • Legal matters subject to solicitor-client privilege
  • Security matters
  • Acquisition or disposition of property

The reason for any closed session will be stated in the meeting agenda, and decisions made in closed session are recorded in the minutes (with confidential details redacted as appropriate).

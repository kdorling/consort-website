Administration Services

January 1, 2025

The Administration Services team works out of the Village Office and serves as the central hub for Village operations. We’re your primary contact point for property tax and utility payments, customer service inquiries, and general information about Village services. Our staff also support Council, manage records and finances, and help coordinate community initiatives. Whether you’re paying a bill, asking a question, or looking to connect with your local government, we’re here to help‚Äîwith a friendly smile and a commitment to excellent service.

What We Do

Administration Services is responsible for:

  • Customer Service: Front-line assistance for residents and businesses
  • Financial Management: Property tax billing, utility billing, accounts payable and receivable
  • Records Management: Maintaining official Village documents and records
  • Council Support: Preparing agendas, minutes, and supporting Council operations
  • Human Resources: Recruitment, payroll, and employee relations
  • Information Technology: Managing Village technology and systems
  • Communications: Public information and community engagement
  • General Administration: Coordinating Village operations and services

Budget Information

The Village prepares an annual operating and capital budget that is approved by Council each year. The budget outlines planned expenditures and revenues for the coming year.

Budget Process:

  1. Administration prepares draft budget (Fall)
  2. Council reviews and deliberates (Winter)
  3. Public input opportunity
  4. Council approves final budget and sets tax rate (Spring)
  5. Budget implemented (Throughout the year)

Financial Reporting

The Village prepares audited financial statements annually in accordance with Canadian public sector accounting standards. These statements are reviewed by an independent auditor and presented to Council for approval.

Freedom of Information & Protection of Privacy (FOIP)

The Village of Consort is subject to Alberta’s Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy (FOIP) Act. Residents have the right to request access to records held by the Village.

How to Make a FOIP Request:

  1. Submit a written request describing the records you’re seeking
  2. Provide your contact information
  3. Pay the application fee (if applicable)
  4. The Village has 30 days to respond

For informal requests for routine information, contact us directly‚Äîa formal FOIP request may not be necessary.

Frequently Asked Questions

When are property taxes due?

Property taxes are due [date]. Penalty interest is applied to overdue accounts. Payment plans are available‚Äîcontact our office to arrange.

How do I set up a new utility account?

Contact the Village Office with proof of occupancy/ownership. We’ll set up your account and provide information about billing cycles and payment options.

What are your office hours?

We’re open Monday to Friday, 8:00 AM to 4:30 PM. We’re closed on statutory holidays.

Can I pay my bills online?

Online payment options are coming soon. Currently, we accept payments in person, by mail, or by phone.

How do I get a copy of a bylaw or policy?

Most bylaws and policies are available on our website. For documents not posted online, contact our office and we’ll provide a copy.

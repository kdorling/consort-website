Protective Services

January 1, 2025

The Protective Services department helps keep the Village of Consort safe, prepared, and well-protected. This includes our dedicated Fire Department, which provides emergency response and fire prevention services, as well as our Emergency Management team, which coordinates preparedness planning and response efforts for natural or man-made events. Protective Services also oversees Bylaw Enforcement, working to ensure community standards are upheld with fairness and respect. Together, these services help create a safe and welcoming environment for all residents.

Fire Safety Information

Smoke Alarms

  • Install smoke alarms on every level of your home
  • Test alarms monthly
  • Replace batteries annually (or as needed)
  • Replace alarms every 10 years
  • Never disable a smoke alarm

Free Smoke Alarm Program: The Fire Department may provide free smoke alarms to residents in need. Contact fire@consort.ca for information.

Carbon Monoxide Alarms

  • Required in homes with fuel-burning appliances or attached garages
  • Install near sleeping areas
  • Test monthly and replace batteries as needed

Fire Extinguishers

  • Keep a fire extinguisher in your home (especially in the kitchen)
  • Know how to use it: P.A.S.S. (Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep)
  • Have extinguishers inspected and serviced regularly
  • Replace if discharged or expired

Home Fire Safety Tips

  • Never leave cooking unattended
  • Keep flammable items away from heat sources
  • Don’t overload electrical outlets
  • Have chimneys and furnaces inspected annually
  • Keep space heaters away from combustibles
  • Never use outdoor equipment (BBQs, generators) indoors
  • Keep matches and lighters away from children
  • Plan and practice a home fire escape plan

Fire Escape Planning

Create a Home Fire Escape Plan:

  1. Draw a floor plan of your home showing all exits
  2. Identify two ways out of every room
  3. Choose a meeting place outside
  4. Practice your escape plan twice a year
  5. Teach children to escape on their own if necessary
  6. If there’s smoke, get low and go
  7. Once out, stay out‚Äînever go back inside

Open Burning Regulations:

  • Burn only during permitted hours

Emergency Management

The Village of Consort’s Emergency Management program ensures our community is prepared to respond to and recover from emergencies and disasters.

Contact Information

What We Do

Emergency Preparedness

  • Developing emergency response plans
  • Training emergency responders
  • Conducting exercises and drills
  • Public education on emergency preparedness
  • Coordinating with regional and provincial agencies
  • Maintaining emergency operations center capabilities

Emergency Response

When an emergency occurs, our Emergency Management team coordinates:

  • Activation of emergency response plan
  • Emergency Operations Center (EOC)
  • Multi-agency coordination
  • Public information and warnings
  • Evacuation procedures (if necessary)

Types of Emergencies

Our Emergency Management program prepares for:

  • Severe weather (tornadoes, blizzards, extreme cold)
  • Flooding
  • Fires (structural, wildland)
  • Hazardous materials incidents
  • Public health emergencies
  • Utility failures
  • Transportation incidents
  • Other natural or human-caused emergencies

Be Prepared

Personal Emergency Preparedness

Every household should be prepared to be self-sufficient for at least 72 hours during an emergency.

Build an Emergency Kit:

  • Water (4 liters per person per day)
  • Non-perishable food (3-day supply)
  • Manual can opener
  • Battery or crank flashlight and radio
  • Extra batteries
  • First aid kit
  • Prescription medications
  • Important documents (copies)
  • Cash
  • Whistle
  • Warm clothing and blankets
  • Personal hygiene items
  • Special items (baby supplies, pet food, etc.)

Emergency Kit Checklist PDF

Make an Emergency Plan:

  • Know the risks in your area
  • Identify safe places in your home for different emergencies
  • Plan how to contact family members
  • Choose meeting places
  • Know evacuation routes
  • Plan for pets
  • Keep vehicle gas tank at least half full

Enforcement Process

The Village takes a progressive approach to bylaw enforcement:

  1. Education: First contact focuses on informing residents about bylaw requirements
  2. Warning: Written warning issued with timeline for compliance
  3. Violation Notice: Formal notice with potential penalty if non-compliance continues
  4. Prosecution: Court action may be taken for serious or repeated violations

Our goal is voluntary compliance whenever possible, with enforcement action reserved for situations where education and warnings are not effective.

Penalties

Bylaw violations may result in:

  • Written warnings
  • Violation tickets (fines ranging from $[amount] to $[amount])
  • Court summons for serious or repeated violations
  • Court-imposed fines up to $10,000 (as permitted by legislation)
  • Orders to remedy the violation
  • Village taking action and charging costs to property owner

Your Rights

If you receive a bylaw violation notice:

  • You have the right to be informed of the specific bylaw provision violated
  • You have the right to respond and provide information
  • You have the right to dispute a violation ticket
  • You may request an extension to comply (in some circumstances)
  • You may appeal certain decisions to Council (where applicable)

Frequently Asked Questions

How high can my grass be?

The Community Standards Bylaw requires property owners to keep grass and weeds cut to a height of [height] or less.

Can I park my RV on the street?

Parking regulations are outlined in the Traffic & Parking Bylaw. Generally, oversized vehicles may have restrictions on street parking. Contact bylaw@consort.ca for specific information.

My neighbor’s dog keeps coming into my yard. What can I do?

Contact Bylaw Enforcement at bylaw@consort.ca or [Phone Number] to report the dog at large. We will investigate and work with the owner.

How many pets can I have?

The Animal Control Bylaw specifies the maximum number of dogs and cats allowed per household. Check the bylaw or contact us for details.

Can I burn leaves in my backyard?

Open burning is regulated by the Fire Services Bylaw. A permit may be required, and fire bans may prohibit burning. Contact fire@consort.ca for information.

Who enforces provincial laws like traffic violations?

The RCMP enforces provincial laws including the Traffic Safety Act. Contact your local RCMP detachment for traffic-related matters on provincial highways.
